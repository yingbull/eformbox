Diagnostic criteria for adult ADHD
A. Part A questions are most predictive of symptoms consistent with ADHD. Part B contains the remaining 12 questions of the DSM-IV-TR criteria.
B. Assess presence of these or similar symptoms in childhood. Adults with ADHD need not have been formally diagnosed in childhood. Look for evidence of early-appearing and long-standing problems with attention or self-control. Some significant symptoms should have been present in childhood.
C. Some impairment from the symptoms is present in two or more settings (eg. work/school, social, and family).
D. There must be clear evidence of clinically significant impairment in social, academic, or occupational functioning. Symptom frequency is often associated with symptom severity. How do these problems affect the ability to work, take care of things at home, or get along with other people such as their spouse/significant other?
E. The symptoms are not part of another mental disorder (eg depression or personality disorder).
